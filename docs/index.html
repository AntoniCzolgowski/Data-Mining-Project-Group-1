Swing State Election Analysis

Identifying High-Volatility Counties and Electoral Patterns

A comprehensive data mining study analyzing Presidential election results (2016-2024) and county-level demographics to identify swing-state counties with the largest voting pattern fluctuations.

Research Topic & Significance

Recent elections at the national level of US government have resulted in poor outcomes for the Democratic party, illustrated by their current absence in the White House and minority status in both houses of Congress. The 2028 Presidential election offers an opportunity to reverse this trend; however, in a polarized political climate, success requires the identification and understanding of the electorate factions that tend to decide national elections within the highest-leverage Electoral states. Our goal is to address this challenge by developing a model that identifies the swing-state counties with largest and most impactful fluctuations in voting patterns between major political parties. Through the integration and analysis of historical Presidential election results (2016-2024) and county-level census demographics (Pennsylvania, Michigan, & North Carolina), we aim to identify counties that offer a combination of likelihood and opportunity for Democratics, as well as the types of voters that contribute to this elasticity. Our analysis will include k-means clustering of counties and association rule mining to uncover the combinations of socio-economic traits that characterize high-volatility electorates.
